A few material facts giving rise to the present appeal are: The appellant owns a poultry farm.
According to the appellant, he used to supply chickens to the complainant and his partner on cash and credit basis.
On 15th June, 2005,<S5> the complainant is stated to have issued a cheque in the sum of rs.8,65,000/- drawn on Union Bank of India in favour of the appellant against the balance payment due.</S5>
</S5>When the cheque was presented for payment, it was returned unpaid by the Bank with the remarks `having no fund’.<S5>
Thereupon, on 7th September, 2005, the appellant served a legal notice on the complainant and his partner in terms of Section 138 of the 2 [Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881](http://www.liiofindia.org/in/legis/cen/num_act/nia1881262/) (for short `the Act'), calling upon them to make payment against the said cheque.
On getting the said notice, <S5>the complainant is stated to have cooked up a story that he had issued blank cheques bearing his signatures to one Salim Ali as security for rs.30,000/- borrowed by him along with a guarantee receipt dated 25th June, 2005 on a stamp paper.</S5>
<S5>Salim Ali misplaced the aforementioned blank cheque, which was fraudulently used by the appellant by filling up the amount of rs.8,65,000/- and was presented to the banker for encashment.</S5>
The <S5>complainant claims to have informed the bank about the loss of the cheque.</S5>

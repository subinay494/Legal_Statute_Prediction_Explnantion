The aforesaid charges were framed pursuant to an occurrence which had taken place in village Kafalta Malla in district Bilaspur in the State of U.P.
wherein 14 persons were killed and 7 injured.
All the 32 accused-respondents were acquitted by the Sessions Judge and, in appeal, the acquittal of all of them was upheld except that accused Kishan Singh, respondent no.20, and Jeet Singh respondent no.14, who were convicted by the High Court under Section 325/34 Indian Penal Code, 1860 and sentenced to five years rigorous imprisonment.
The occurrence in question took place on 9th May, The complainant's side is Dom by caste.

<S1>Before examining the evidence of the eye-witnesses we may note the undisputed facts.</S1>
<S1>On 9th May, 1980 an occurrence took place in the village of Kafalta Malla.</S1>
<S1>Most of the residents of the said village were Thakurs of Brahmins.</S1>
<S1>The only house of a scheduled caste in that village was that of Nari Ram, PW- The occurrence took place when the complainant's party reached the said village taking a doli in which the bridegroom was sitting.</S1>
<S1>Even if the story with regard to the four ladies stopping the doli is not accepted as correct, it is not in dispute that near the house of Nari Ram, to which the doli had proceeded, some words were exchanged between Khima Nand (deceased) and Divani Ram, PW- A large number of villagers had collected there.</S1>
<S1>According to the complainant, Khima Nand took out a knife and there was a scuffle between him and diwani Ram and during this scuffle the father of the bridegroom, namely, Mohan Ram tried to snatch the knife.</S1>
<S1>Dewani Ram was injured and Khima Nand was also injured, but he succumbed to those injuries after a few days.</S1>
<S1>According to PW 1 to PW 5, PW 7 and PW 9 some of the villagers shouted that they should kill and burn the Doms and it was thereafter that the marriage party was attacked.</S1>
<S1>Some of the members of the marriage party took shelter in the house of Nari Ram, but that house was set on fire which resulted in the death of five of them inside the house, while the body of another person was found in the courtyard of Nari Ram's house which was half burnt.</S1>
<S1>Apart from these six deceased, eight other persons were killed and their bodies were found laying in the adjoining fields of the village.</S1>
<S1>the nature of the injuries on the deceased and the assailants, except for the injury on Khima Nand (deceased) and, Possibly Diwani Ram, were all caused by blunt instruments or stones.</S1>
Except for Khimanand, no other villager was injured.
<S1>On the other hand, except for those members of the marriage party who ran away from the scene of occurrence, when the riot started, none were spared.</S1>
<S1>Nevertheless as per the evidence of all the eye witnesses, a large number of villagers had gathered there and they had with them lathis and sticks.</S1>
<S1>With Khima Nand being injured, all hell broke loose.</S1>
<S1>A cry was raised that the doms should be burnt and killed, and this is precisely what happened.</S1>
<S1>the marriage party was assaulted by the villagers.</S1>
<S1>Six of the members of the marriage party were burnt, five of them having been locked inside the house of the only Dom resident of the village whose house was also burnt.</S1>
<S1>Eight others were pursued and then mercilessly beaten and were killed elsewhere in the village.</S1>
<S1>Considering the number of injuries on the persons who had died, it is evident that a large number of persons must have taken part in the assualt.</S1>
<S1>All the eye witnesses have said that fifty or more villagers had taken part in the attack.</S1>
<S1>Who were members of the assembly will be considered later but what is relevant to note is that a large number of villagers were present, duly armed with lathis and sticks, when the occurrence started and except six people who were burnt, eight others were beaten to death by blows from lathis, sticks an stones.</S1>
<S1>Out of the seven eye witnesses PW 1 Narendra Parsad and PW 7 Diwani Ram named all the 28 respondents, other than four ladies, as being the assailants who were part of the unlawful assembly.</S1>
PW3 Ganga Ram identified, apart from the four ladies, 26 of the respondents as having attacked the marriage party.
<S1>What, in our opinion, is the most important evidence with regard to the burning of the Nari Ram's house is the testimony of Nari Ram PW8 himself.</S1>
<S1>He has deposed that his was the only scheduled caste Family in the village and he has stated, in no uncertain terms, that he witnessed the act of the burning of his house.</S1>
<S1>He stated that he was coming back from his field at about 6 P.M.</S1>
<S1>when he heard the noise in his court-yard.</S1>
<S1>He saw Dan Singh respondent no.
1 & Ram Singh respondent no.
10 who were spraying kerosene oil on his house.
According to him respondent no.
23 Jasod Singh and respondent no.
24 Gusain Singh were putting the house on fire from below by putting the light.
He found his wife and children standing outside in the court-yard and they were crying.</S1>
<S1>Some of the processionists, who were members of the marriage party were inside the house and they had been locked from outside.</S1>
<S1>The house, as well as his belonging inside, were burning and at that time "swarnas injured me also".</S1>
<S1>He stated that he was injured as a result of the lathis blows and stones being thrown on him.</S1>
<S1>The High Court failed to appreciate that in his evidence Nari Ram has stated that when his house had been set on fire he and his family members hid themselves in the adjoining house of Keshi Ram.</S1>
<S1>They stayed in that house the whole night and he further deposed that because of fear he did not come out of keshi Ram's house even the next morning.</S1>
<S1>In this state of fear and dread in which Nari Ram was on the day following the burning of his house if he did not mention to the Investigating officer the names of those persons who had set his house on fire, the same cannot be regarded as surprising.</S1>
<S1>As already noticed, Nari Ram's was the only scheduled caste family in the village consisting of Thakurs and Brahmins.</S1>
<S1>These Thakurs and Brahmins had attacked the marriage party of the Doms and had killed fourteen of them.</S1>
<S1>Apart from PW 1 and PW 7 who had named all the respondents as being the assailants, respondents Dan Singh, Ram Singh, Jasod Singh and gusain Singh had been identified by PW 8 and other eye witnesses also as being part of the unlawful assembly.</S1>
<S1>Other eye witnesses who had identified these four persons, as being part of the unlawful assembly, though they have not been specifically named as being the persons who set Nari Ram's house on fire, are PW 2, PW 3, PW 5 and PW 9 who had identified Dan Singh and Ram Singh and PW 3 who had identified Jasod Singh and Gusain Singh.
it is true that Nari Ram has not named the persons who attacked him with sticks and stones but the very fact that he was attacked and he suffered injuries shows that at the time of the occurrence he was present at his house and, therefore, he must have seen the assailants specifically named by him, We have read his evidence with care and we see no reason as to why he would wrongly name only four persons out of a mob of more than sixty, as being responsible for burning his house with the five Doms and all of his belongs being inside.</S1>
<S1>Eight other person were killed by the members of the unlawful assembly at different places in the adjoining fields.</S1>
<S1>it is obvious that there must have been a fairly large number of assailants who could have chased and then beaten eight different people to death at different place.</S1>
<S1>Till the entire incident came to an end with the killing of these fourteen persons, the unlawful assembly which came into existence immediately on the injury of Khima Nand continued.</S1>
<S1>The members thereof would be liable for the killing of these persons, even if it is not possible to ascribe the overt acts of each of the assailants.</S1>
<S1>Nari Ram had stated that he had come to his house after it had been set on fire.</S1>
<S1>Having worked as a teacher in the school, one can safely presume that he would be in a position to identify at-least some of the villagers who were assembled there.</S1>
<S1>Further more, he himself had suffered a number of injuries and it is difficult to accept that in an incident where fourteen people were killed and seven injured, including PW 1 himself, he would not have seen anyone taking part in the attack.
Merely because he had not mentioned in the First Information Report the names of the persons who had set on fire the house of Nari Ram, can be no ground for rejecting his testimony.</S1>
PW 1 Narendra Parshad, after going back to his villager, wrote out the First Information Report in which the entire incident which had occurred was mentioned in which all the respondents were implicated.
This report was given to the patwari at 10 P.M.
on the night of the incident.
the mentioning of the names of all the respondents in this report soon after such a serious incident had occurred, does clearly indicate that PW1 Narendra Parsad must have been able to identify at-least some if not all the person named therein.

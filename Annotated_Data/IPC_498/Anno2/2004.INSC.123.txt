
The case of the prosecution is that the wife of the appellant, namely, <S6>Jeeto Rani committed suicide on 1986 on account of the cruelty and harassment meted out to her by the appellant</S6> herein.

Whenever Jeeto attempted to prevent her husband from taking 'Bhang' <S6>he used to be assaulted by him.</S6>

The informant alongwith his brothers and others reached village Kheri and found that <S6>his daughter was dead.</S6>
In the report he stated that <S6>he entertained a suspicion that Jeeto had committed suicide by taking poison being fed up by the beatings and the harassment caused to her by her husband.</S6>
In the FIR only two allegations were made by Munshi Ram, PW-2, firstly, that <S6>there were frequent quarrels, sometimes resulting in physical assault</S6>, between the appellant and Jeeto on account of his being addicted to consumption of 'Bhang', and secondly, that the appellant was aggrieved by the fact that his sister was not being properly looked after by his brother-in-law namely, Fateh Chand, PW- Munshi Ram was examined by the prosecution as PW- In his deposition he stated that the appellant was addicted to liquor and bhang and <S6>whenever Jeeto attempted to persuade him to desist from this addiction he used to misbehave with her and even beat her.</S6>
Fateh Chand, PW-3 also deposed in favour of the prosecution and he also alleged that the appellant was addicted to liquor and bhang and that <S6>he had been told by Jeeto that the appellant did not want to keep her as he did not find her to be good looking.</S6>
According to Fateh Chand, PW-3 whenever <S6>Jeeto came to their house she used to complain about the treatment meted out to her by the appellant.</S6>
One fails to understand why the appellant should make such an allegation when his sister was living happily with Fateh Chand, PW- As to the frequent assaults on the deceased by the appellant and her reporting the matter to her father and brother, there appears to be no reason why, if these facts were true, no such allegation was made in the course of investigation by the prosecution witnesses PWs 2 and We are, therefore, satisfied that the prosecution has been able to establish its case only to the extent that the <S6>appellant was addicted to 'Bhang' which was opposed by his wife Jeeto and on account of such opposition there used to be frequent quarrels and may be on some occasions Jeeto was assaulted by the appellant.</S6>
